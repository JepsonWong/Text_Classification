Effects of Glucose Polymer Beverage on Performance of Young vo
lunteers
Chen Jidi et al
(Institute of Sports Medicine, Beijing Medical University, Be
ijing, China 100083)


Abstract To unit_test the effects of a sport beverage containing 20%
glucose polymer and adequate concentration of electrolytes, 
12 males and 12 females (aged 21-26) were randomly distributed i
nto 3 groups (n=8) to participate in a cycling unit_test at the intensi
ty of 70% HRmax. 150ml glucose polymer drinks (A), sugar drinks (B) 
and placebo drinks (C) were consumed before and every 20 minutes 
during exercise. The subject was diagnosed as fatigue when the 
heart rate reached 76% HRmax and quitted cycling. Results show
ed glucose polymer drinks to be of benefit potential in maintai
ning blood glucose, blood volume, plasma insulin, serum potas
sium and magnesium at physiological homeostatic levels, mini
mizing the increased event of blood lactic acid and enhancing e
ndurance performance.
Key words glucose, polymer, beverage, caloric intake, metabo
lism, homeostasis, endurance
